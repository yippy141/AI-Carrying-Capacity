# V0 Methodology

Status: public draft for the first AI Conversion Atlas content layer.

Evidence status: unscored. Country and sector values remain missing until reviewed source rows and indicator methods are promoted into the canonical data layer.

## Research Question

AI Conversion Atlas asks a narrower question than "who is ready for AI?" Given access to a particular level of AI capability, how effectively can a society convert that capability into useful outcomes in firms, laboratories, infrastructure, public services, security institutions, and daily life?

The V0 pilot starts with the United States and China because the strategic question is sharpest there: if the United States reaches the frontier first, does that automatically create durable national advantage, or can China realize more benefit from somewhat weaker systems in selected domains because of stronger deployment machinery? This is a hypothesis to test, not a conclusion to assume.

## Why This Is Not An AI Readiness Index

AI readiness indices are useful context, but they usually blend inputs that the Atlas needs to keep separate: digital infrastructure, talent, governance, research output, firm adoption, and policy intent. The Atlas is designed as a conversion diagnostic, not a national trophy table. ## Unit Of Analysis

The main unit is a country-sector or country-constraint module. The first sector module is manufacturing, robotics, and embodied AI. The first cross-cutting constraint module is compute, data centers, electricity, and grid absorption.

The Atlas can show country-level views, but those views are assembled from modules. A country may have strong frontier access, weak local energy absorption, fast industrial diffusion, narrow benefit distribution, and unclear realized outcomes at the same time.

## Five Layers

| Layer | V0 meaning | What it must not be confused with |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Frontier access | The level of AI capability that actors in a country can build, buy, import, host, adapt, or use. | Real deployment, productivity, welfare, or military advantage. |
| Conversion capacity | Complementary assets that turn AI into repeated operational use: compute, power, data, sensors, standards, integrators, management, capital, procurement, and domain expertise. | General optimism, policy ambition, or raw model quality. |
| Adaptation capacity | The ability of institutions, labor markets, law, infrastructure, and legitimacy systems to absorb disruption. | Public enthusiasm, low regulation, or rapid rollout alone. |
| Distribution quality | Whether benefits diffuse across workers, firms, regions, citizens, suppliers, and public services rather than concentrating among frontier firms, elites, or state actors. | Aggregate output growth by itself. |
| Realized outcomes | Observed changes in productivity, industrial capability, scientific output, service quality, defense capacity, welfare, and adjustment costs. | Adoption rates, investment flows, or pilot announcements. |

Diffusion speed is tracked inside the conversion chain as the time from pilot to repeated, intensive, value-producing use. It is not treated as a separate virtue unless outcomes and harms are also visible.

## Evidence Labels

Every claim, indicator, and visual state should carry one evidence label.

| Label | Use |
| --- | --- |
| observed | Directly measured in a credible dataset, law, filing, audit, survey, or research design. |
| official-claim | Claimed by a government, public agency, company, or program owner but not independently validated. |
| qualitative-coded | Coded from a documented rubric using named sources. |
| estimated | Estimated using a transparent formula, inputs, and uncertainty note. |
| missing | Not available, not reviewed, not comparable, or not yet safe to show. |

Official claims can be important evidence of policy direction or state capacity, but they are not the same as independently validated outcomes.

## Missingness Policy

Missing values stay visible. They are not filled with regional averages, model guesses, or qualitative impressions unless an explicit estimate is documented and labeled.

If a country has a strong narrative but weak evidence in a module, the correct V0 display is "hypothesis pending evidence," not a score.

## Source Rules

Wikipedia is not used as an evidence source. V0 prioritizes government strategies, laws, regulations, official statistical datasets, intergovernmental datasets, peer-reviewed papers, academic working papers, think tank reports, technical standards, grid-operator materials, corporate filings, and named expert essays from reputable AI researchers. Expert commentary may shape scenarios, but it is not treated as empirical evidence unless it contains data or a formal method.

Sources are classified by type and reliability tier before they support any indicator value. The source register must distinguish official government claims from independent empirical findings.

## Indicator Policy

Indicators should be narrow enough to preserve interpretation. For example, "industrial robot density" belongs in conversion capacity; "manufacturing productivity" belongs in realized outcomes; "large-small firm AI adoption gap" belongs in distribution quality; "frontier model production" belongs in frontier access.

Candidate values remain missing until the source, unit, geography, time period, and comparability limits are documented. ## China-US Hypothesis Discipline

The Atlas treats China-US advantage as domain-specific and conditional. One country can lead in frontier model capability while another converts adequate models faster in a particular sector. One country can build infrastructure quickly while facing weaker feedback, openness, or allocation quality. One country can have stronger private innovation while bottlenecked by local permitting, grid queues, labor scarcity, or fragmented adoption.

The V0 question is therefore not "who wins AI?" The question is: where does frontier access turn into outcomes, where does it stall, who benefits, who bears adjustment costs, and which constraints are binding under different scenarios?

## V0 Publishing Rule

V0 can publish source-backed narratives, missing-value maps, hypothesis cards, and uncertainty-aware visuals. It should not publish a national ranking, a composite national score, or a claim of China or U.S. advantage without source-backed module evidence.
