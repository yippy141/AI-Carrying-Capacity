## Why This Module Comes First

Manufacturing, robotics, and embodied AI are the first sector module because they turn the abstract AI race into physical conversion problems: machines, plants, supply chains, workers, standards, maintenance, and operational outcomes. The module tests whether AI capability becomes measurable improvements in production rather than remaining a software demo, policy target, or investment story. This module should not be read as a generic automation ranking. A country can have high robot density and weak AI integration. A country can have strong frontier AI and weak plant-level diffusion. A country can have many smart-factory labels without comparable evidence of productivity, quality, safety, or wage outcomes.

## Conversion Chain

The manufacturing conversion chain runs from frontier or adequate models to industrial data, machine vision, sensors, robots, controllers, digital twins, industrial standards, systems integrators, cybersecurity, maintenance networks, trained workers, workflow redesign, and observed operational results.

The key question is not whether a factory uses "AI" somewhere. The key question is whether AI is integrated into repeated production decisions with measurable effects on cycle time, quality, downtime, scrap, customization, throughput, safety, energy use, inventory, or resilience. ## Measurement Traps

Do not count robots as AI outcomes by default. Robots are a conversion-capacity anchor; realized outcomes require operational evidence.

Do not compare smart-factory counts across countries unless certification standards, maturity levels, and measurement units are aligned.

Do not treat domestic robot production as equivalent to robot adoption.

Do not treat one successful showcase factory as national diffusion.

Do not treat labor exposure as job loss. Pair exposure with wages, employment, mobility, training, and safety-net evidence.
