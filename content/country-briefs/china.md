# China Country Brief

Status: V0 draft. No scored country assessment.

## Core Hypothesis

China may be able to convert somewhat weaker AI systems into large real-economy deployment in selected domains if state coordination, manufacturing scale, infrastructure buildout, industrial policy, and public-service platforms reduce the distance from pilot to rollout. This is a hypothesis, not a finding. ## Layered Read

### Frontier Access

China should not be coded as simply "behind" or "ahead" without model-family, benchmark, chip, and deployment-context evidence. ### Conversion Capacity

The China conversion hypothesis is strongest where AI can be inserted into existing industrial, infrastructure, platform, or public-administration channels. Manufacturing, robotics, industrial internet, smart terminals, logistics, healthcare administration, education platforms, and local-government services should be evaluated separately. TODO_SOURCE: needs official Chinese ministry citations and independent validation for AI-plus deployment channels, industrial-internet programs, smart-factory categories, public-service platform scale, and sectoral AI pilots.

Compute and electricity should be treated as a conversion-capacity test, not just a national resource statistic. The relevant question is whether compute hubs, grid planning, firm power, cooling, water, latency, and chip access align with actual workloads. TODO_VERIFY: confirm latest policy status and implementation evidence for East Data West Computing, national integrated computing network plans, green data-center rules, provincial compute hubs, and power-grid coordination.

### Adaptation Capacity

China's adaptation capacity should be measured through labor transition policy, skilling systems, occupational standards, public tolerance, complaint channels, social insurance, local fiscal capacity, and the state's ability to correct deployment failures. Official training targets and employment-friendly AI language are not enough by themselves. TODO_SOURCE: verify with MOHRSS materials, national and provincial skilling policies, labor-market data, AI governance rules, complaint-channel requirements, and independent labor research.

### Distribution Quality

V0 should ask whether AI gains reach SMEs, inland regions, lower-tier cities, workers outside frontier firms, public-service users, and exposed occupations. Large platform scale or high public optimism should not be treated as broad distribution without evidence on access, quality, affordability, and remedies. ### Realized Outcomes

Outcome claims should be limited to observed changes in productivity, quality, cost, availability, scientific throughput, public-service delivery, industrial capability, military capability, welfare, or adjustment costs. Official smart-factory counts, AI-plus targets, platform-user totals, or demonstration zones belong in conversion capacity or diffusion until outcome evidence is reviewed. ## Measurement Traps

Do not treat official targets as outcomes.

Do not treat public-service platform scale as AI service quality.

Do not treat high deployment tolerance as durable legitimacy.

Do not treat manufacturing scale as AI conversion unless AI-specific use and outcomes are visible.

Do not treat state coordination as unambiguous advantage; it may speed rollout while weakening feedback, transparency, or allocation quality.
